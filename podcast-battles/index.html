Podcast Battles

My Final Version

AI Generated Version

The original idea behind our podcast was to talk about (mostly negative) effects of generative AI in general, exploring societal, environmental, and cognitive issues. While researching, we decided this was far too much to naturally fit into a 10 minute window, and narrowed it down to a cognitive / educational scope. I liked the research that we did, and the actual content in the podcast was good and aligned with our idea. I feel like the delivery could have been a little better. Thinking in hindsight, some of the genuine conversations between my partner and I while researching would have probably fit better into the podcast than what we actually recorded for it.


Creating the AI version was surprisingly difficult. As one of our sources was significantly larger than the others, Google's NotebookLM decided it was the only relevant source, and completely ignored the others in its creation. Trying to do anything in a "Debate" format led to hilarious results, featuring Character A reading statistics directly from the study, and Character B outright accusing them of lying while making up nonsensical rebuttals... for 14 minutes straight. It really solidifies my claim in our version about how AI should not be used "on it's own" / without an intermediary. We ended up generating a script with ChatGPT, voicing it using a free ElevenLabs model, and editing it together to ChatGPT's exact specifications.


As for a comparison, the human and AI versions of the podcast turned out surprisingly similar. Our podcast started with the negative effects of AI, as they are most prominent in the traditional school format, and then pivotted to talking about its positive applications, and how the issues may be alleviated by properly adopting AI in the classroom and teaching students how to use it "correctly" instead of cutting it out entirely. The AI podcast started by talking about AI usage as a tutor in classrooms, almost as if it were already widely adopted, and then addressed briefly how misusing it could be harmful at the end.


The AI version shared our weakness of not really being able to simulate a conversation, and maybe the lesson to be learned from this is that that's very hard, if not impossible in a work where all of the lines were prepared ahead of time. Even when the answers to questions weren't scripted, they still seemed like they were as we had ample amounts of time to think about them and internalize a response. If I were to do this project again, maybe I would have my partner and I research our topics in complete isolation, and then actually discuss what we learned blindly for 10-20 minutes on recording.
